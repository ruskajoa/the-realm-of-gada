# The Gods of Gada
The category of "god" is a falsehood. There is no single element connecting these otherworldly beings - only their shared otherworldliness. But to a mortal, this is connection enough.

The gods of Gada are of two kinds. The ancient, eternal beings who form the world and the universe around it, ever-present, ever-changing yet always dormant, are known as the [[The Gods of Gada#The Numen\|Numen]]. The Numen *are* the very realms and the universe themselves, their corporeal bodies together forming the realms, the heavens and the stars in an interconnected embrace. They exist wholly in the [[Observable World\|Observable World]]. The presence of the Numen is unquestionable - as matter-of-fact as the existence of oceans and mountains. The Numen have existed since the dawn of time, far before any civilization, and will continue to exist far beyond the collapse of the last empire.

And then, along came civilization, and with them came the [[The Gods of Gada#The Pantheon\|Pantheon]], and with the Pantheon came the [[Unobservable World\|Unobservable World]] - a mystical place beyond any realm or plane where gods take shape from the collective dreams of civilization. These are the gods that people worship, and they influence the realms from their ineffable homes in the Unobservable World. The Pantheon lives and dies, ebbs and flows according to their presence in civilization.
# The Pantheon
The Pantheon are the gods birthed from and worshiped by civilization. Some have existed since the dawn of civilization, the very first ideas people ever thought to idolize - others came later, as civilization developed and found new ideas to revere. The Pantheon therefore tends to increase in size in tandem with the progress of civilization, as new gods form from new inventions, discoveries, philosophies. Rarely has a god of the Pantheon died, but if a god's importance wanes in civilization, that is the road down which it is headed. The Pantheon are incorporeal, as opposed to the Numen, and reside entirely within the [[Unobservable World\|Unobservable World]], influencing the realms from there.
## Elder Gods
Elder Gods are deities of the Pantheon who represent ephemeral, mystic forces intrinsic to civilization. They have become so ubiquitous through millennia of worship that they have become unforgettable to the world, and cannot die. These gods rarely meddle in mortal affairs.
#### Affilan, Weaver of the Arcane
Affilan is the god and creator of arcane magicks, and the being which feeds the energy of all arcane magic in Gada.
#### Devos, Divine Mage
Devos is the god and creator of divine magicks. Faith and worship of them is what gives divine magic its energy and charge.
#### Væthen, God of Kings
Væthen is a god associated with power, influence and lordship. Rulers and highly influential figures are considered "The Favoured of Væthen," and in human cultures the right to rule is considered to come from him. Another title Væthen is sometimes known by is "The Tyrant," as rulers have a lively history of invoking his will to justify imperialism, oppression and various atrocities.
#### Miere the Dreamweaver
Miere is the goddess of dreams and the aether, and is often associated with rest, creativity, and spirituality. The astral realm is seen as Miere’s body, in the same way that mountains and forests are seen as the bodies of dwarven and elven gods. Some cultures consider nightmares to be a warning or ill omen from Miere, and similarly pleasant dreams are seen as a blessing. Miere is also associated with death, graves often reading the epitaph: “May you rest in Miere’s embrace.”
#### Eir-Aloi, Goddess of Health and Healing
Eir-Aloi is the wellspring of all good health and healing in the realms, often called on to bless meals and make them healthy, or prayed to by herbalists and medicinists as they make healing potions and items. Places of healing have a special connection to Eir-Aloi, as they believe her healing energy is what enables clerics and the like to cast healing magic and perform healing rituals. It's common for settlements in the realm to have some place of worship for Eir-Aloi that functions as a place of healing. Be it a shrine or an entire cathedral, this is usually the largest and/or most important religious building in the settlement.
#### Vanæsir, God of Cosmic Space
When civilization first discovered portals and realm travel, Vanæsir sprung forth rapidly from this discovery to govern and monitor cosmic space and travel through it. He beholds all the movement within cosmic space and prevents cosmic disasters from happening.
#### High Goddess Deraneyr
Deraneyr, High Goddess of the Dwarves, is known as the “Mother of Dwarves,” the originator of all dwarves in Gada. She is associated with creation in the sense of construction and craft. Dwarves specifically consider these things akin to birth, and in dwarven religious circles Deraneyr has that association also. She is also a goddess of wisdom and pride, inspiring these important values in dwarves.
#### High Goddess Elviathar
Elviathar, High Goddess of the Elves, is known as a warrior guardian of nature, and many warrior spirits and guardian spirits take on her likeness - a tall, strong woman whose hair flows three times the length of her body. She nurtures the soil beneath Eremas forest, giving it growth and the people within protection. Her association is that of protection, parenthood, strength and magical prowess.
## House gods
The only differences between House Gods and Elder Gods are ubiquity and specificity. House gods are not so widely worshiped as to have become unforgettable to the world, due largely to their domains being far more specific than those of Elder Gods. House Gods represent philosophies, lifestyles, civilizational advancements - specific things that people have discovered or created themselves.

House gods tend to be the primary target of worship for people such as clerics, paladins, warlocks and the like, and they indeed tend to meddle in mortal affairs frequently and heavily. People tend to keep idols of them in their homes - thus the name "house gods." Many holy places will also have wings, altars and figures dedicated to house gods of importance to them in particular.
#### Barnyk the Sculptor
“Her likeness is in every statue, every machine and every building.” A dwarven god, Barnyk is associated with art, craft, and patience. She is also closely tied to the processes of creation and building, embodied in the smallest of trinkets and the grandest of cathedrals. Barnyk is the favoured deity of artists, builders, tinkerers and inventors. Artificers tend to have her as their house god to bless their inventions - mainly in the hopes that they won’t explode.
#### Æthelred, God of Oaths and Promises
Vows of eternal love, oaths to sacred duties - Æthelred’s is the name invoked in these rituals. Æthelred is a favoured deity amongst paladins and knights. He is also invoked in many militaries to remind the soldiers of their duty to their monarch, and in wedding ceremonies to ensure the couple’s vows are binding.
#### Diröst the Wretch
Misery and morbidity, darkness and depression, the squalid lives of urchins and vagrants - these are the things that keep Diröst, god of wretchedness and misery, alive. They are not commonly known to be worshiped as a house god, let alone in any churches, but the outcasts and the downtrodden often find some comfort in this miserably deity.
#### Gratanomisara, God of Luck and Chance
A coin flip in the form of a god. Gratanomisara represents all the things people can only explain with luck or chance, and is seen as something of a trickster god. An uncommon deity for anyone’s house god, but adorns the walls of gambling halls.
#### Arassimon, Goddess of Wealth and Fortune
Wealth, prosperity, good fortune and success are associated with the two-headed Arassimon, her two heads representing the two sides of wealth - a boon that enriches lives and leads to prosperity, but also a burden that turns the mind towards avarice and snobbery. Whatever their motivations for it, those who wish for great wealth will often have Arassimon as their house god.
#### Belvadér, Divine Duke of Heroism, Adventure and Bravery!
The bold and noble hero of any adventurer or thrill-seeker, Belvadér embodies the heroic virtues of bravery, adventurousness, freedom and duty to others. Adventurer's guilds nigh-universally display shrines and paintings of Belvadér in their halls, as do many knights and old war heroes in their homes. Belvadér is often used as a role model for children so they'll learn and live by the heroic virtues, starring in many fables and myths, and he is a beloved deific figure in cultures that glorify adventure.
#### Lady Lavender of Love and Lust
Pleasures of the flesh and head-over-heels infatuation are the realm of Lady Lavender. Hers is no humble love, but a fiery, passionate, loud and boastful desperation for one another, making her a popular house god among kinksters, lovers, bards and brothels.
# The Numen
The Numen are not gods in the traditional sense. They are the corporeal components of the [[Observable World\|Observable World]]; the stars, the skies, the realms. Dear reader, you may liken their existence to how mountains, planets and galaxies exist in our universe. The Numen exist across all [[Planes of Existence\|planes of existence]] simultaneously.
## The Celestials
The Celestials are gods so great and ineffable that they can only be seen in the stars. The constellations in the night sky together form the faces of the Celestials, who look down upon the world from far beyond. Their names are the oldest in all of Gada, named in a tongue called "Odarinn," oft called the Tongue of the Stars. No word of this language has been spoken in millennia - only the names of the Celestials remain.

## Those in the Heavens
The Celestials sent their emissaries to create the Realms - these emissaries are called Those in the Heavens. They are the skies, natural forces and atmospheres around realms, and created Those on Earth - corporeal forms of the realms themselves. If the Celestials are to be seen as the constituent parts of the universe, Those in the Heavens may be seen as the constituent parts of solar systems.
#### Lóra, the sun
Lóra is the sun which gives Gada light and heat - the very thing that makes life possible. She fostered the growth of the world’s nature, and the cycle of day and night, and the passage of time.
#### Lúna, the moon
Luna is the moon, that which commands the oceans and the tides of Gada.
#### Astór, the aurora
#### Sefír, the wind
#### Lúvia, the rain
## Those on Earth
Created by Those in the Heavens, Those on Earth are the corporeal forms of any given realm - in this case Gada. They are the mountains, rivers, oceans, forests, plains, deserts, caves, volcanoes of the natural world, all bound together in embrace to create a realm. If Those in the Heavens are to be seen as the constituent parts of solar systems, Those on Earth may be seen as the constituent parts of planets - or realms, as they are called here.
#### Dæra, the earth
Dæra is the earth of Gada, his [[The Face of Dæra\|face]] and [[The Hands of Dæra\|hands]] forming its two main continents. Two more of Dæra's hands reach out from the sea, although he is believed to have seven in total. Dæra is often represented in illustrations as a compass, with his hands pointing west, south and east, and his face pointing north.
