# *Dreyfig Varaði* - the Unjust Vengeance
In Dwarven historical accounts, the [[Second Scourge\|Second Scourge]] is called the Unjust Vengeance. Dwarves were especially targeted by the ancient dragons who coveted the great riches found in their mountains, as well as the ingenious weaponry built by the dwarves, which the dragons wanted for their own plans of warfare and conquest. The dragons would run dwarves away from their mountain homes and claim them for themselves, far more often than they would destroy the homes of others. For this reason, dwarves hold a uniquely personal hatred for the dragons which isn't quite shared by all Elteveldtians, and a unique account of the Second Scourge also.

The dwarves see the Second Scourge as revenge from the dragons, as the ancient dragon [[Skagerrach\|Skagerrach]] had been defeated in the [[First Scourge\|First Scourge]] by a Dwarven-made weapon. Also influencing this view of the Second Scourge as vengeance is the destruction of [[Dordunmir\|Dordunmir]], the famed Dwarven mountain capital which glistened on the mountainside, high above Elteveldt, and the occupation of the Dwarven tomb of [[Dwarfsrest\|Dwarfsrest]] in the eastern mountains. The Second Scourge, more than anything felt like a targeted attack on the dwarves of Elteveldt.

*Dreyfig Varaði* is remembered by dwarves in the Song of the Second Scourge - or, in the Dwarvish tongue, *Hymna dyr Dreyfig Varaði*. Indeed, much of Dwarven history is recorded in song, as singing was a hallowed custom of the dwarves. *Hymna dyr Dreyfig Varaði* is one of the most solemn and sad of the [[Dwarven songs\|Dwarven songs]], and is sung only in trusted company.
