# The Banishing of the Dragon
The Banishing of the Dragon was an age-defining event in the history of [[Elteveldt\|Elteveldt]], marking the first day of the first year of Elteveldtian timekeeping. It happened in early spring, during the Thaw, in the month that came to be known as Tŵiresong. Ever since, the Elteveldtian calendar begins at the first sign of the Thaw in spring.

Before the banishing, Elteveldt was inhabited by its indigenous people, Elteveldtians, and dragons, an invasive force from the [[Barrowlands\|Barrowlands]] who the Elteveldtians struggled against for several millennia during the [[History of Elteveldt#Dragon Age\|Dragon Age]]. This age was defined by an endless cycle of violence wrought upon the Elteveldtians by the dragons, and much of the culture that developed during and before this age has only been recorded in songs and spoken tales.

While the dragons grew comfortable and arrogant in their reign, the Elteveldtians fought and organized tirelessly - in the end, they developed magic and weaponry mighty enough to fell the terrible dragons consistently, and the tides of the millennia-spanning war were finally turned. The final few centuries of the Dragon Age are known as the Vanquishing of the Wand'ring Foe, culminating in one final onslaught where the Elteveldtians finally drove the last great dragons out of their lands, and the lesser dragons and wyrms fled in tow over the eastern mountains. This event came to be known as the Banishing of the Dragon, and is celebrated on the first day of every year.

Following the Banishing of the Dragon, Elteveldtians rejoiced and celebrated for long, and the land and its people began to prosper. The Dwarves especially, whose ancestral mountain homes had most been occupied by the dragons, prospered greatly. The era that followed the Banishing of the Dragon is indeed called the [[Era of Dwarven Prosperity\|Era of Dwarven Prosperity]], which saw the Dwarves greatly enriched with mountain gems, build their mountain capital of [[Dordunmir\|Dordunmir]] and the great forge above it, and create there mighty weapons and spells. This Era lasted until the [[Second Scourge\|Second Scourge]], known by the Dwarves as *[[Dreyfig Varaði\|Dreyfig Varaði]]*, the Unjust Vengeance.
