# History of Elteveldt
Elteveldtian timekeeping begins at the [[Banishing of the Dragon\|Banishing of the Dragon]], when the long conflict between Elteveldt's indigenous peoples and the invading dragons came to an end as the dragons were finally banished to the [[Barrowlands\|Barrowlands]] over the eastern mountains. The banishing of the ancient dragon [[Skagerrach\|Skagerrach]] marked the beginning of the first year of the New Age, abbreviated N.A., from which time has since been kept and back-calculated according to the Elteveldtian calendar. The previous age is called the Dragon Age (or Age of Dragons), abbreviated D.A. (or A.D.), which ended in the year 4983 D.A.

A number of distinct eras followed from the Banishing of the Dragon. The [[Era of Dwarven Prosperity\|Era of Dwarven Prosperity]] began soon after the Banishing of the Dragon, and saw Dwarven cultures and kingdoms thrive greatly across the land. The great mountain capital of [[Dordunmir\|Dordunmir]] was built, and above it, near the peak of the mountain, a great forge in which the dwarves of yore made mighty creations.

The Era of Dwarven prosperity was interrupted in 276 N.A. with the return of the ancient dragon Skagerrach, which began the [[Era of the Scourges\|Era of the Scourges]]. Skagerrach attacked alone in the [[First Scourge\|First Scourge]], which at the time was simply called "the Scourge," and wrought havoc on Elteveldt for many years until a legendary band of heroes defeated Skagerrach by sealing him in a prison created by the dwarves. Four hundred years hence, the rest of the ancient dragons attacked in full force in 676 N.A., bringing an end to the Era of Dwarven prosperity as they reduced Dordunmir to smoldering ruins and forced the dwarves to leave their home city and become a diaspora.

Another band of adventurers answered the call to vanquish the dragon invaders, and they were able to defeat the ancient dragons - but not without casualties. Skagerrach was freed from his prison, and sundered the land once more, eternally marking it by his destruction. This was called the [[Second Scourge\|Second Scourge]], and ended the Era of the Scourges. However, many years later, the glistening citadel of [[Endalin\|Endalin]] was built around the corpse of Skagerrach.

After the Second Scourge began the [[Golden Era\|Golden Era]]. The dragons were defeated, forevermore to stay away from Elteveldt, and people began focusing less on the prospect of calamitous warfare and more on trade, craft, philosophy and politics.
