# The Face of Dæra
The earthen Numen god [[The Gods of Gada#Dæra, the earth\|Dæra]] forms all the physical land in Gada with his body. His face, breaching the surface of the deep sea, spans millions of kilometres southward from the northern pole of the realm. His eyes form great lakes, and his mouth a gaping abyss between the northernmost part of the continent and the rest of it, opening to the wide ocean to the south.

The Face of Dæra splits into several regions by geography, known as lands. These are not to be confused with nations, city-states or other constructs of civilization which ebb and flow over time. Over the Mouth of Dæra to the east lies the fabled land of [[Elteveldt\|Elteveldt]], where magic is potent and ley-lines converge. Beyond the eastern mountains of Elteveldt lie the [[Barrowlands\|Barrowlands]], home of the Dragons ever since the [[Banishing of the Dragon\|Banishing of the Dragon]]. Due south of Elteveldt are the [[Solemn Plains\|Solemn Plains]].

North of the Mouth of Dæra is a land of high mountains and bitter winters known as the [[Hinterlands\|Hinterlands]]. These mountains separated the eastern part of the continent from the west until the first seafaring civilizations came along. In the west, the vast land of [[Xanthia\|Xanthia]] swallows Dæra's north eye and spans all the way to the western coast. The lands of [[Vedallún\|Vedallún]] and [[Cascadia\|Cascadia]] hug the southern coast of the continent, and the islands of [[Anned-þem\|Anned-þem]] dot the ocean from the northwestern coast of the continent to the far north.
