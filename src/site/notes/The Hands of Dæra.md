# The Hands of Dæra
The corporeal body of the Numen god [[The Gods of Gada#Dæra, the earth\|Dæra]] forms all the earth-matter on Gada. Of this body, only Dæra's [[The Face of Dæra\|face]] and three of his hands breach the surface of the ocean, the corporeal body of the Numen god [[The Gods of Gada#Môrin, the sea\|Môrin]].

The three hands reach out heavenward from points in the sea far away from each other. Seafarers have long used these hands to orient themselves on their voyages as two of the hands lie conveniently east and west from Dæra's face and third hand.
## The eastern hand
The eastern hand of Dæra rises high into the sky, meeting the ocean surface just above the wrist. The hand is cupped towards the sky with its six fingers curling upwards. The palm is practically habitable cradling a vast land of hills and forests, albeit nigh-impossible to reach without flying. Even the ocean around the wrist is too tempestuous for any ship to brave, and thus the eastern hand has remained largely uninhabited.
## The western hand
The western hand of Dæra is mostly submerged, only its seven fingers rising sharply out of the ocean like collapsing towers. These fingers are sheer rock where very little grows, and the ocean slowly erodes them on the palm-side.
## The southern hand
The southern hand of Dæra lies almost flat against the ocean surface, knuckle-side to the heavens, curled slightly so that its five fingers dip back into the sea and create a gargantuan cave between his palm and the ocean surface. Vast mountain ranges and valleys line the habitable side of the southern hand, which is home to many lands and civilizations.
